Manchester United Football Club, founded in 1878 as Newton Heath LYR, has one of the most illustrious and storied histories in football. The club changed its name to Manchester United in 1902 after being saved from financial troubles by local brewery owner John Henry Davies. United’s early successes included their first league title in 1908, followed by an FA Cup win in 1910, signaling the club’s potential. However, it wasn’t until the 1950s and the rise of manager Sir Matt Busby that the club began to truly dominate English football. Busby, known for nurturing young talent, led United to several league titles and built the famous "Busby Babes," a team of young stars that seemed destined for greatness. Tragically, in 1958, the team was struck by the Munich Air Disaster, which claimed the lives of eight players and left the club devastated. Yet, under Busby’s leadership, United rebuilt and achieved European glory by winning the European Cup in 1968, becoming the first English club to do so.
